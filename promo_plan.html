5. On "Bot Comments" — Why Not, and What Instead

Do not use bots for bookstore reviews. Here's why:
  • Detection risk: Bertrand/Wook (Porto Editora group) and FNAC moderate reviews. Multiple reviews from new accounts, similar writing style, or same IP range will get flagged.
  • Penalty: Book can be flagged, reviews removed, and in worst case the listing gets deprioritized in search results.
  • Reputation: João is a cybersecurity professional. If fake reviews are discovered, the irony would be devastating for his credibility — the exact audience he targets (security, defense, policy) would notice.
  • Unnecessary: The book has a real audience. The Culturgest launch was packed. Real reviews from real readers are achievable.
What to do instead — Organic review generation:
  • Email the launch attendees — "Thank you for attending. If you've started reading, a short review on Bertrand/Wook would mean a lot." Include direct links.
  • LinkedIn ask — Post: "The book is out for a week. If you've read it, I'd love an honest review on [store]." The professional network will respond.
  • Panelist endorsements — Ask Álvaro Beleza, João Massano, etc. to post their honest take on LinkedIn. One senior endorsement > 50 fake comments.
  • Review copies — Send 5-10 free copies to journalists, academics, bloggers who cover PT geopolitics. Ask for nothing — just send with a note.
  • Goodreads — Create the book page, mark it as "currently reading" yourself, invite network. Goodreads reviews are public and boost discoverability.

Custom AI Agents (we build and deploy)

Agent 1: Content Generator PRIORITY 1

Claude API + Python | Runs weekly | Output: 8-12 posts/week

Batch-generates 4-6 weeks of Portuguese social media content from book themes, chapter excerpts, and the 4P framework. Tone-locked to João's voice (trained on his LinkedIn posts and SOL interview).

  • LinkedIn: Long-form thought leadership posts (500-800 words)
  • Instagram: Captions, story text, carousel copy, quote card text
  • Variation: Each theme generates 3 variants for A/B testing
Replaces 10-15 hours/month of writing. João reviews and posts — the AI drafts, the human approves.

Agent 2: News Hook Monitor PRIORITY 1

RSS feeds + Claude API | Runs daily 7 AM | Output: email digest

Scans Portuguese news every morning for topics João can comment on, linking to his book framework. This is how nonfiction authors stay in the news cycle — reactive PR.

  • Sources: Observador, Expresso, Público, DN, Lusa, RTP news feeds
  • Triggers: Sovereignty, cybersecurity, NATO, defense spending, EU reform, multipolar, migration
  • Output: "Today's hook: [headline]. Angle: tie to Chapter X, 4P pillar Y. Suggested LinkedIn post draft."
One timely commentary post = 5-10x engagement vs scheduled content. This agent ensures João never misses a news hook.

Agent 3: Podcast Pitch Writer PRIORITY 2

Claude API + web research | Runs on demand | Output: personalized emails

Researches each target podcast (recent episodes, host style, guest history), then generates a personalized pitch email in Portuguese — from João's perspective, emphasizing why he's the right guest for that specific show.

  • Input: Our 10-show hit list from Section 6
  • Output: Personalized email + 3 suggested talking points per show
  • Extras: One-page media kit PDF, author headshot package
10 personalized pitches in 20 minutes vs 4-5 hours manually. João sends from his own email for authenticity.

Agent 4: Review & Mention Tracker PRIORITY 2

Web scraping + Claude API | Runs daily | Output: weekly report

Monitors all bookstore product pages and social media for reviews, ratings, and mentions. This is the ethical, useful alternative to the "bot comments" idea — know when real people talk about the book, then engage authentically.

  • Monitors: Bertrand, Wook, FNAC, Goodreads, Amazon.co.uk product pages
  • Social: Instagram hashtags (#nacaovalente), LinkedIn mentions, Twitter/X
  • Output: Weekly summary — new reviews, sentiment analysis, notable mentions, suggested responses
Catches every review and mention within 24 hours. Enables João to thank reviewers personally — building community, not faking it.

Agent 5: Ad Copy Optimizer PRIORITY 3

Claude API | Runs before each ad cycle | Output: 10-15 ad variants

Generates and scores Meta ad copy variations before spending money. Each variant gets a headline, body text, and CTA optimized for a specific audience segment.

  • Audience-specific: Defense/security hook vs diaspora hook vs general citizen hook
  • Format-specific: Feed ads, Story ads, Reels captions — different copy for each
  • Scoring: Rates each variant on clarity, emotional hook, CTA strength before launch
Testing 5 copy variants instead of 2 = finding winners faster, lower CPC. Pays for itself in avoided ad waste.

Agent 6: Review Request Sequencer PRIORITY 3

Claude API + email templates | One-time setup | Output: 3-email sequence

Generates a timed email sequence for Culturgest attendees and João's network who bought the book. Personalized, warm, and timed to reading progress — not spammy bulk emails.

  • Day 1: "Thank you for your support at the launch"
  • Day 10: "How are you finding it? I'd love to hear your thoughts"
  • Day 21: Gentle review request with direct links to Bertrand/Wook/Goodreads
This is the ethical version of "bot comments" — real reviews from real readers, at scale. 20-30% of recipients typically leave a review.
